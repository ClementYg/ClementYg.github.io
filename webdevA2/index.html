Astrology

"Sky and earth together produce omens Each separate, not divided; sky and earth are interconnected. A sign which is bad in the sky is bad on earth; a sign which is bad on earth is bad in the sky" translated by Francesca Rochberg, a historian specialised in ancient Mesopotamian.

Mesopotamia

Celestial Omens were recorded as conditional statements (if __ in the sky moved, something in earth changed)

750 BCE

Astrology was state-supported and reports would be sent to the king by scholars. This was called Mundane Astrology as astrological omens were taken to indicate things about cities, nations and large groups of people.

5th Century BCE

Zodiac standardised with 12 signs of 30 degrees each Ephemerides produced to study movements of planets using advanced mathematical astronomy. At this point, astronomy and astrology was interconnected as there was no real difference between the two subjects yet Mesopotamia was conquered, decentralising astrology

410 BCE

Natal Astrology was introduced where birth charts started being used, based on planet's placement when ~~ was born, under premise that this affects their quality of life and future Birth charts contain raw data which calculates where the positions of the planets were when the person was born, but it does not actually tell you much.

Egypt

Decans were developed as a tool for telling time and timing religious rituals. Mesopotamians were primarily focused on the movement of the ecliptic, which is essentially the zodiac, the Egyptians were more focused on what we call the diurnal rotation, the spinning of the Earth and the day cycle.

2nd Century BCE

Similar to Birth Charts, the Egyptians focused on the rising decan and where each decan fell relative to it at the moment of birth. They associated a topic to each decan, i.e 28th decan was associated with livelihood The decans were speculated to be the concept of the 12 houses, which associated the signs of the zodiac with topics relative to the rising decan.

Unification

Summarising the conquests of Alexander the Great, Mesopotamia and Egypt were now under the control of Greek-rulers, Greek then became the lingua franco, allowing the unification of Egyptians and Mesopotamians. Zodiac and Egyptian decans start being merged in respective temples.

100 BCE

Hellenistic astrology emerged after the conquests of Alexander coming from Egypt. This astrology system represents the establishment of the 'fourfold' system that we use in modern-day, as well as the synthesis of Mesopotamian and Egyptian astrological traditions. - The planets and most things we associate with them - the signs of the zodiac and qualities we associate with them - the concept of 12 houses and their basic meanings - the concept of aspects, The Hellenistic astrology system represents two frameworks being merged - The movement of the planets along the ecliptic - the 24-hour cycle of the movement of the planets in the diurnal rotation Egyptian decans were now overlaid on top of zodiac, where it became 36 10-degree subdivisions of the signs of zodiacs, with 3 decans in each sign. The rising zodiacal sign came from the rising decan at the moment of birth and treating that as important and numbering them based of the subdivisions The rising zodiacal sign was then attributed to represent the 1st house of the native, and the rest of the signs were numbered orderly based after the 1st house. This is the whole sign house system

1st Century

Roman Empire takes over Egypt and reaches its peak by 2nd century CE, where Hellenistic astrology also seems to the at its peak.

2nd Century

Hellenistic astrology is transmitted to India, merging with preexisting astrology in India including the lunar zodiac nakshatras, starting a tradition of astrology there over the next 2000 years.

7th Century

Western Roman Empire fell and Astrology stops being practiced in Western Europe as much, with general decrease in learning and increased hostility towards astrology from Christianity. Hellenistic Astrology had stopped in general due to other advents and events occurring in the world. More importantly, the [[Horary Astrology]] became relevant and established as part of the tradition. Electional Astrology is founded based of the moment of time a question is asked, for important events or personal questions such as the best date for marriage Four traditional Branches of Astrology - Mundane Astrology - Natal Astrology - Electional Astrology - Horary Astrology

8 - 12th Century

European kingdoms took land back from Islamic rulers, translating ancient texts and reviving the practice of astrology since its death during the Roman Empire Astrology, especially electional and horary, was now being used for stuff such as medicine, time of illness and health.

17th Century

The Astrological Recession -- Astrology declines in the West due to social, scientific and religious changes

20th Century

Rebirth of astrology, attributed to another change in culture where spiritualism and the Theosophical Society grew. People were more accepting and found it fashionable to talk about karma, reincarnation and things alike, leading to renewed public interest A lot of astrology's popularisation can be contributed by Alan Leo, who made sure it was easier to access and understand. Astrology being more about prediction in traditional astrology to more about character analysis. After the birth of depth psychology in the 20th century, it eventually centered around psychological analysis and introspection. 1930s onwards, Sun-sign astrology was popularised and astrological columns focused on the zodiac was published in newspapers and magazines Generally, shift in 20th century as its more on deep psychology and character depth as opposed to predictive fatality or life. More innovation > tradition Outer planets were found, thus integrated to replacing the more general placements traditionally. I.e, Uranus replaced Saturn as the ruler of Aquarius Increased accessibility due to computers and easy calculation without prior knowledge Techniques were rediscovered, using whole sign houses and sect to propose a redevelopment of traditional key ideas of astrology. Going back into ancient texts, techniques may give us greater insight on development and innovation of astrology.

Polarity

It is just a shortcut way to explain how some of the signs characteristics express themselves. Nothing more, nothing less (no, positive signs are not automatically "good", nor are negative signs automatically "bad"). Every sign has a polarity. Sometimes called positive/negative, masculine/feminine, or other variations of the same thing (yin/yang, so on). In general, positive signs are more outgoing, expressive, forceful, and proactive, while negative signs are generally more introspective, slightly reserved, perhaps, and reactive, or passive.

Modality

Modalities are qualities or modes of expression. as the tropical zodiac is tied to the seasons, each zodiac sign is associated with its seasonal stage CARDINAL signs “initiate” - they are enterprising, action-oriented, & begin each season. Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn. Cardinal energy can sometimes struggle with the follow-through. FIXED signs “maintain” - they are stabilizing, persistent, & sustain the season. Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius. Fixed signs can be stubborn & set in their ways. MUTABLE signs “adapt” - they are versatile, flexible, & end the season. Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces. Mutable signs can be restless & inconsistent, or feel guilty for doing all the things.
